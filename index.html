The Process of Conducting a Contextual Interview

Step 1:

Set up a conventional interview with your subject

This can be anywhere, although if you're unsure, a coffee shop is always a safe bet

Step 2:

Make your subject comfortable. Use small talk, and get them to act as naturally as possible

Step 3:

Transition into requesting a contextual interview

Don't worry if you get rejected! Just thank them for their time, and try elsewhere

Step 4:

Prepare questions for the contextual interview

Make sure they are pertinent to what you are trying to answer

Step 5:

Conduct the contextual interview

Make sure the subject acts as naturally as possible, and don't get in their way

Step 6:

Debrief with the subject

Clarify any questions or concerns you have

Step 7:

Consolidate and record thoughts and ideas

Write down anything you find important before you forget them, and organize your notes
